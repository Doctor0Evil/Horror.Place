# Rivers of Blood Charter

## Version: 1.0
## Effective Date: 2026-04-01

### 1. Core Doctrine: Implication Over Explicit Depiction

The Horror$Place ecosystem operates under a fundamental principle: **Fear is generated through implication, evidence, and procedural revelation, not through explicit graphic content.** This charter defines the boundaries of acceptable horror expression and the safety tiers governing content distribution.

### 2. The Doctrine of Procedural Implication

All horror content must adhere to the principle of "procedural implication." This means:

- **Evidence-Based Fear:** Horror is conveyed through discoverable, interpretable evidence (e.g., archival logs, spectral static, biometric residue, ledger fragments, ritual stains).
- **Absence as Presence:** The most terrifying elements are those absent from direct view but strongly suggested by contextual clues.
- **Curated Ambiguity:** Stories and experiences must maintain a level of ambiguity that allows multiple interpretations, preventing easy dismissal and encouraging speculation.

### 3. Safety Tiers and Content Classification

Content within the Horror$Place ecosystem is classified into three distinct with specific guidelines and access controls:

#### 3.1. Standard Tier (Tier 1)
- **Target Audience:** General audience with an interest in atmospheric, implication-based horror.
- **Content Guidelines:**
  - No explicit violence or gore.
  - No direct depictions of human suffering.
  - Horror arises from atmosphere, sound, environmental storytelling, and evidence of past events.
  - Subject matter may include historical tragedies, urban legends, and mythological themes handled abstractly.
- **Distribution:** Public repositories (e.g., `Horror.Place`).

#### 3.2. Mature Tier (Tier 2)
- **Target Audience:** Adults seeking deeper, more intense horror experiences grounded in historical or realistic contexts.
- **Content Guidelines:**
  - May include abstracted or heavily stylized representations related to historical atrocities or trauma, always presented through the lens of evidence and implication.
  - Increased focus on psychological unease and cognitive dissonance.
  - Subject matter can explore darker historical events, complex folklore, and more unsettling implications, but must remain within the bounds of the Doctrine of Procedural Implication.
- **Distribution:** Private, user-gated repositories (e.g., `HorrorPlace-Codebase-of-Death`, `HorrorPlace-Black-Archivum`).

#### 3.3. Research Tier (Tier 3)
- **Target Audience:** Authorized researchers and developers working on advanced horror mechanics and high-fidelity simulations.
- **Content Guidelines:**
  - Highly experimental content designed for research purposes.
  - May include extreme examples of implication-based horror for study and development.
  - Strict adherence to ethical guidelines and consent protocols for any human-subject research components.
  - Access limited to authorized personnel with specific research clearance.
- **Distribution:** Private, research-access-only repositories (e.g., `HorrorPlace-Process-Gods-Research`, `HorrorPlace-Redacted-Chronicles`).

### 4. Violation Protocol

Any content found to violate the principles of the Rivers of Blood Charter will be subject to immediate review and potential removal or revision. The core invariant systems (`H.` API) and style validation tools (`StyleLintRuntimeValidator`) are designed to automatically detect and flag potential violations based on schema and contract definitions.

### 5. Continuous Evolution

This charter is a living document. It will be reviewed and updated periodically based on community feedback, technological developments, and the evolving understanding of responsible horror creation within the VM-constellation framework.
