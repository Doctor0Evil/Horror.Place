# machine-canyon.md

## Style Overview: The Machine-Canyon
**Machine-Canyon** is a visual manifestation of "Functional Horror"—a post-apocalyptic aesthetic where the line between industrial machinery and biological life has been violently blurred. In this world, survival is an anomaly. The environment is not a setting, but a digestive system: an endless, vertical chasm of grinding gears, weeping pipes, and parasitic pistons that have repurposed the "organic" as a fuel source.

> *"Nature did not lose to the machine; it was integrated into the machine's bill of materials."*

---

## 1. Core Visual Pillars

### 1.1 Biomechanical Parasitism
The machine does not merely kill; it absorbs. The style emphasizes **implied consumption**. 
* **Asset Logic:** You will not find corpses, but you will find a leather boot fused into a hydraulic press, or human hair acting as a friction-reducing gasket in a gear assembly.
* **The "Pulse":** Surfaces are not static. Walls may slightly expand and contract (respiration), and pipes vibrate with the rhythmic thrum of a heartbeat that isn't quite mechanical.

### 1.2 The "Canyon" Geometry
The environment is defined by extreme **verticality and claustrophobia**.
* **Narrow Pathing:** Walkways are rarely wider than a human torso, forcing a constant proximity to the "living" walls.
* **Abyssal Depth:** Looking up or down reveals no sky or floor—only a vanishing point of swaying cables, steam, and rotating turbines.
* **Non-Euclidean Hints:** Corridors may subtly shift. A door that was behind the viewer may now lead to a dead-end intake vent.

## 2. Technical Palette & Materials

The color theory is rooted in **Industrial Decay** and **Visceral Oxidation**. All saturation is kept below 50% to maintain a sense of oppressive gloom.

| Element | Material/Color | Hex Code | Visual Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Structure** | Oxidized Copper | `#3B4D4D` | Heavy verdigris; metal that looks "bruised." |
| **Shadows/Fluids** | Oil Black | `#050505` | Viscous, iridescent sheen on floors; deep voids. |
| **Status/Warning** | Amber Glow | `#FFBF00` | Dim, flickering filament bulbs; "unhealthy" light. |
| **The "Input"** | Dried Blood Rust | `#4A2C2A` | Metal corrosion that mimics scabs or dried ichor. |

---

## 3. Lighting & Atmosphere

### 3.1 Artificial Tyranny
There is no natural light. All illumination is generated by the machine itself, serving its own functional needs rather than human visibility.
* **Hard Shadows:** Light sources are sharp and singular (arc-welding sparks, single hanging bulbs), creating long, distorted shadows that hide the "shifting" geometry.
* **The Strobe Effect:** Lights flicker in sync with the environment's "respiration," creating a disorienting visual rhythm that mimics a failing BCI (Brain-Computer Interface).

### 3.2 Atmospheric Density
The air is a character. It is thick with:
* **Suspended Particulates:** Floating soot, shredded fiber (clothing remnants), and steam.
* **Inward Suction:** Vents do not blow air; they pull it in, dragging light debris toward unseen consumption zones.

---

## 4. Design Constraints (The "Do's and Don'ts")

| **DO** | **DO NOT** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Show specialized tools fused into walls. | Show open skies or horizons. |
| Use "breathing" animations for heavy metal. | Use bright, high-saturation colors. |
| Emphasize the sound of hydraulics as "screams." | Show explicit gore or dismembered bodies. |
| Make every path feel like a trap or an intake. | Include sentient AI or "friendly" robots. |

---

## 5. Emotional Resonance: The "Grit"
The Machine-Canyon evokes a sense of **terminal insignificance**. The horror stems from the realization that the machinery isn't malevolent—it is simply indifferent. It is a world of gears that will keep turning whether a human is caught in them or not. The "steampunk" elements (brass, steam, analog gauges) are stripped of their whimsy and replaced with a cold, grinding inevitability. 

The viewer should feel like a **foreign object** inside a clock that is slowly learning how to grind them down into oil.
