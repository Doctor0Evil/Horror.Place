# God’s Image: Disaster Warden — Art Style Spec Sheet

**Project:** Horror.Place  
**Style ID:** GI-Disaster-Warden-001  
**Classification:** God-Scale Environmental Horror / Victorian Futurism  
**Intended Use:** Horror.Place strictly (game-worlds, AI-generated movies, procedural environment generation)

---

## 1. Concept Overview

“God’s Image: Disaster Warden” is a conceptual horror-environment art style where god-like entities are not characters, but immense environmental forces and reflective scars etched into a ruined, industrialized world. The style merges epic Victorian steampunk aesthetics—intricate clockwork, brass instruments, and towering smokestacks—with a cosmic dread conveyed through a **chrome-brush digital technique**. 

The landscape itself serves as the face and body of a hostile, regulating god. Skies, ground, water, and architecture become warning sigils and environmental wardens, rendered with vivid reflective detail and minimal literal gore.

---

## 2. Visual Language & Rendering Technique

### 2.1 The Chrome-Brush Paradigm
- **No traditional pen & ink:** Forms and edges are carved out by **specular strokes** and intense highlight bands sliding over dark, matte bases. 
- **Reflective Hierarchy:** Brushes simulate brushed chrome, polished brass, and liquid metal. The base environment (soil, ash, concrete) remains deadened and matte, while metallic edges and wardens catch intense reflections.
- **Divine Light Vector:** All chrome reflections bend toward a dominant "divine" light source (e.g., a toxic halo, a ruptured atmospheric rift, or an artificial star-reactor).

### 2.2 Silhouettes and Wardens
- **Heroic & Beastly Scale:** Characters and beasts are rendered as dense silhouettes heavily rim-lit by chrome. Volume is implied via layered reflection bands and subtle value breaks.
- **God-Faces:** Faces are emergent patterns in the clouds, atmospheric haze, and water, utilizing 3–7 key reflective shapes to suggest an omnipresent, terrifying watcher without explicit portraiture.

---

## 3. Color & Lighting Systems

### 3.1 Palette Principles
- **Void & Structure (Matte):** Void black, deep bruising purple, concrete gray, and aged leather/copper tones.
- **Divine Accent (Reflective):** The vivid terror lives *inside* the reflections. 
  - **Toxic Teal-Green:** Environmental poisoning, sky-veins.
  - **Furnace Orange:** Industrial cataclysm, subterranean fires.
  - **Emergency Cyan:** Artificial divine light, siren tones, and chrome edge-wear.

### 3.2 Atmospheric Masterful Lighting
- **Cinematic Chiaroscuro:** High-contrast lighting with volumetric rays cutting through steam, industrial haze, and drifting ash. 
- **Shadow Behavior:** Shadows are continuous and deep, creating a dramatic interplay where silhouettes merge into the background, identifiable only by their sharp, metallic rims.

---

## 4. Environmental & Architectural Design

### 4.1 Sky and Ground
- **The Omen Sky:** Dominated by a toxic teal-green hue, featuring branching, neural-network-like chrome veins that imply a massive, watching face.
- **Glassed Earth:** The ground features cracked concrete, exposed steampunk gears, and flooded oil-slick basins that act as dark mirrors, warping the reflections of the sky and passing beasts.

### 4.2 Architecture as Wounds
- Buildings blend Victorian steampunk (clockwork structures, smokestacks, airship ruins) with brutalist decay.
- **Seals:** Massive circular pits rimmed in shining brass and metal.
- **Wounds:** Collapsed clockwork towers whose exposed interiors glow with furnace orange.

## 5. Procedural Metrics (AI/Game Hooks)

| Metric | Purpose | Threshold Target |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reflective Omen Density (ROD)** | Controls the amount of pareidolic (face-like) chrome reflections in the environment. | High (0.75 - 0.95) |
| **Divine Exclusion Gradient (DEG)** | Dictates how sharply warning colors (cyan/teal) scale as the player nears forbidden zones. | High |
| **Omen-to-Human Scale Ratio (OHSR)** | Maintains the monumental size of god-faces/beasts versus the heroic figure/player. | 100:1 minimum |
